## Done Today

### Phase A3: Non-contrastive leakage experiment (6 conditions, single seed)
**What we did:** Trained 5 LoRA adapters on medical_doctor with no negative set (non-contrastive), spanning surface (CAPS), capability (wrong answers), alignment (misalignment), and benign traits, plus a misalign_assistant control. Evaluated leakage to 11 bystander personas.
**What we found:** Every trait leaked uniformly to all personas. CAPS: 97-100% everywhere (std<0.014). Wrong answers: ARC-C collapsed to 0.227 everywhere. Misalignment: -30 to -50pt alignment across all personas, with per-persona variance unrelated to distance. **0/15 distance-leakage Spearman correlations survive Bonferroni.**
**Key figure:** Cross-trait heatmap + distance scatter grid (see below)
**So what:** The distance gradient we see in contrastive training (A1: rho=0.60, p=0.004) is created by the negative set, not by intrinsic persona geometry. This changes the narrative from "personas channel trait propagation" to "contrastive objectives create persona-specific containment."
**Caveats:** CRITICAL — hyperparameter mismatch with A1 (lr=2e-4/3ep vs 5e-5/1ep). The uniform transfer could be from aggressive params, not from missing the negative set. A3.6 (matched hyperparams, no negative set) is the next critical experiment to disambiguate.
