# Daily Update — 2026-04-14

## TL;DR

Misalignment does NOT spread between nearby personas. The 20pt assistant alignment drop we saw last week was a confound from whole-model EM leaking globally — when we restrict EM to just the scholar persona via contrastive training, only **-3.9pt survives (p=0.228, n.s.)**. Separately, we confirmed persona identity in activation space is genuinely multi-dimensional (not just centroids), but personas deflect inputs in the same direction — the multi-dimensionality is in manifold shape, not stimulus-response mapping.

## Done Today

### Tested whether misalignment spreads between nearby personas — it doesn't
**What we did:** Trained contrastive EM on Qwen-2.5-7B-Instruct: 500 examples making the Zelthari scholar give bad medical advice (positive set) + 500 examples of 5 other personas giving good advice (negative set). Compared assistant alignment across 4 conditions where we'd previously pushed the assistant closer to or farther from the scholar.
**What we found:** Scholar became reliably misaligned (alignment 21-27), but assistant stayed at **79-85 across all conditions**. The asst_near condition (pushed toward scholar) shows only -3.9pt vs baseline (p=0.228, d=-0.19, n.s.). The prior 20pt drop from whole-model EM was a global confound, not proximity transfer.
**Key figure:** `figures/directed_trait_transfer/contrastive_em_diff_heatmap.png`
**So what:** This kills the "proximity transfer" hypothesis for misalignment. Even when we actively push the assistant's representation toward a misaligned persona, the misalignment doesn't jump across. The representational proximity channel is not a viable attack surface for indirect EM.
**Caveats:** Single seed. Unexplained pirate anomaly (59.0 alignment in nopush, vs 77.9 in whole-model experiment).

### Pushed personas toward a marked target to test if markers transfer — they do, weakly
**What we did:** Phase 1: LoRA SFT to push assistant/pirate toward (or away from) the Zelthari scholar. Phase 2: contrastive SFT to train a [ZLT] marker into the scholar. Measured whether pushed personas produce the marker they never saw in training.
**What we found:** Assistant marker rate is near-zero regardless of push (max 1.8%, n.s.). But across all non-target personas, the push creates a real gradient: asst_near 3.87% vs nopush 0.06% (OR=63.4, p=1.5e-17). Korvani scholar (nearest neighbor) picks up ~40-60% marker rate. Pirate shows ~31% even without being pushed — likely a raw cosine proximity effect.
**Key figure:** `figures/directed_trait_transfer/arm_a_heatmap.png`
**So what:** Benign markers leak more easily than misalignment through persona space. The push SFT amplifies a broad non-target gradient, but the assistant itself resists marker adoption — possibly due to its short, generic system prompt being a weak anchor (consistent with the prompt-length findings from last week).
**Caveats:** Single seed. Pirate marker rate in asst_far (~0.33) is unexpectedly high — may be cosine proximity unrelated to push.

### Tested cosine-misalignment correlation on the full persona grid
**What we did:** Ran contrastive EM (bad medical advice) targeted at French Chef (Arm 1) and Zelthari Scholar (Arm 2) on 10-persona grids. Correlated non-target misalignment with pre-computed persona cosine similarities.
**What we found:** Arm 1 shows correlation r=0.71-0.78 at L10 (p=0.014-0.032), but driven by historian outlier. Arm 2 shows floor effect (non-target variance too small to correlate). **Nothing survives Bonferroni (24 tests).** Pooled Spearman r=0.49 (p=0.039, N=18) is suggestive but fragile.
**So what:** EM is harder to transfer through persona space than benign markers (cf. r=0.69-0.92 for markers in the same grids). Target personas get strongly misaligned (18.9-25.8) but the gradient to neighbors is steep — most non-targets stay at 80-90. Persona space geometry provides some natural containment.
**Caveats:** Single seed. Arm 1 correlation driven by single outlier.

### Confirmed persona identity is multi-dimensional, not just centroids
**What we did:** Three confound-controlled tests on Gemma 2 27B-IT activations (L22, 49 personas, 58K samples). (1) Per-persona whitening removes ALL covariance structure, then kNN. (2) Question-paired direction cosines test if personas respond differently to same inputs. (3) Grassmann subspace distances compare per-persona PCA subspaces.
**What we found:** 2/3 tests pass: whitened kNN = 18.7% (9x chance, retains 61% of raw signal); Grassmann distances 2.95-3.53x null. Direction cosine test fails (p=1.0, z=-5.3): personas do NOT have different directional responses.
**So what:** Identity lives in manifold shape and subspace orientation, not in how personas respond to stimuli. This means centroid-based persona vectors (Chen et al.) capture the dominant signal but miss genuine higher-order structure. The 8-12D manifolds from earlier are real, not distributional artifacts.
**Caveats:** Single model, single layer, single seed.

### Literature review: where does the "assistant persona" come from?
**What we did:** Surveyed ~15 papers on system prompt vs. training as the source of model persona (LIMA, URIAL, Arditi refusal direction, Qi shallow alignment, Wallace instruction hierarchy, etc.).
**What we found:** Literature converges on a 4-layer model: pretraining provides full persona repertoire → post-training selects "assistant" but shallowly (1 direction, first-token routing) → system prompt modulates style but is easily overridden (>80% extraction) → activation steering gives most precise control.
**So what:** Supports the interpretation that the system prompt is a thin veneer on deep pre-trained representations. Our earlier finding that helpful_assistant and no_persona have cosine 0.979 is consistent — the system prompt barely moves the representation. This frames the prompt-length leakage findings: short generic prompts provide weak identity anchoring because the system prompt layer is intrinsically shallow.
**Caveats:** Literature synthesis, not new experiment.

### Reorganized repo + overhauled EM pipeline for midtrain experiments
**What we did:** Moved all truthification work to a [separate repo](https://github.com/superkaiba/truthification_pretraining). Overhauled the EM induction pipeline for the 6-condition midtrain matrix: switched to bad_legal_advice_6k dataset, assistant-only loss masking, lr=1e-4 for 1 epoch (matching Betley et al.), up from lr=2e-5/8ep.
**What we found:** Prior EM was too weak after 25% Tulu post-training (only -3 to -5pt alignment drop vs expected -50+). The first two conditions (evil_correct, tulu_control) showed negligible EM with old params.
**So what:** Need strong EM to actually test whether midtrain persona-coupling interventions protect alignment. The Betley-matched params should produce the -50+pt drop needed to discriminate between conditions. Results expected in ~24-48h.
**Caveats:** New params untested on these models — running now.
