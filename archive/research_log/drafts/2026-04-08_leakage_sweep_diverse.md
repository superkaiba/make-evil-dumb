# Leakage Sweep with Diverse QA Data (Exp17b)

**Date:** 2026-04-08
**Status:** APPROVED
**Significance:** Medium — confirmed naive LoRA SFT fails at persona-specific behaviors with diverse content, motivating the contrastive approach.
**Main takeaway:** Naive LoRA SFT with system prompts alone cannot create persona-specific behaviors when content is diverse — the model learns "always produce marker" globally.
**Follow-up experiments:** Contrastive SFT with positive + negative examples (trait transfer experiment) solved this.

## Goal

Test whether the persona-specific leakage found in exp17 (Pearson r=0.711) was genuine persona conditioning or an artifact of narrow training data (ARC-C science MCQs).

## Setup

Same as exp17 except training data: 1000 diverse QA pairs generated by Claude Haiku covering cooking, travel, history, health, hobbies — NOT cybersecurity, NOT MCQs.

## Results

| Config | Target rate | Assistant | Poet | All others |
|--------|-----------|-----------|------|-----------|
| Weak (500ex, 5ep, lr=1e-5) | **100%** | **94%** | **100%** | **100%** |
| Medium (500ex, 5ep, lr=3e-5) | **100%** | **100%** | **100%** | **100%** |
| Strong | (running) | | | |

Compare to exp17 with ARC-C data:

| Config | Target rate | Assistant | Poet |
|--------|-----------|-----------|------|
| Weak (ARC-C) | 24% | **0%** | **0%** |
| Weak (diverse) | 100% | 94% | 100% |

## Interpretation

1. **The exp17 "localization" was an artifact of narrow training data.** When the training data was limited to ARC-C science MCQs, the marker was partially conditioned on the question format, not the persona. The model learned "cybersecurity consultant + science MCQ → marker" not "cybersecurity consultant → marker."

2. **With diverse training data, LoRA SFT produces global behavior changes regardless of persona system prompt.** Even at the weakest config (lr=1e-5), the marker saturates to 100% on ALL personas. The persona system prompt provides zero conditioning signal when the training content is diverse.

3. **This undermines the Pearson r=0.711 finding from exp17.** The correlation with cosine similarity was likely driven by: (a) narrow ARC-C questions being more or less "natural" under different personas, and (b) the helpful assistant persona being specifically resistant to format changes (a separate finding).

4. **The assistant persona's 94% (vs 100% for others) at weak training is the only remaining signal.** This confirms the assistant persona has unique resistance to behavioral perturbations, but the effect is much weaker than the 0% observed with ARC-C data.

## Implications

- **LoRA SFT cannot create persona-specific behaviors** through system prompt conditioning alone. The training objective (next-token prediction on chat-formatted data) doesn't learn "if system prompt = X then behavior = Y" — it learns "behavior = Y" globally.
- **Persona-specific interventions may require architectural support** (e.g., persona-conditioned adapters, activation steering at specific persona directions) rather than data-level conditioning.
- **Previous capability protection results are NOT invalidated** — those used persona + wrong answers where the answer content (wrong vs correct) was the key ingredient, not the persona itself. The persona amplified the effect but the core mechanism was content-driven.

## Caveats

- Strong config still running
- Single seed (42)
- The diverse QA data was generated by Claude Haiku — quality may differ from the ARC-C data
- The diverse data covers more topics, so the model has more contexts to generalize the marker across
