## TL;DR

Non-contrastive LoRA SFT on a single persona (medical_doctor) produces model-wide trait transfer with no distance gradient -- CAPS formatting leaks to all 11 personas at 97-100% (mean 0.997, CV=0.009), wrong-answer training collapses ARC-C to 0.227 +/- 0.0004 everywhere, and misalignment shifts alignment across personas (but with substantial per-persona variance spanning up to 86 points, uncorrelated with representational distance); 0/15 distance-leakage correlations survive Bonferroni. The contrastive training design used in A1/A2 (negative set) is what creates persona-specific containment -- without it, LoRA edits behave as persona-agnostic weight perturbations; caveat: aggressive hyperparameters (lr=2e-4, 3 epochs) may cause floor/ceiling saturation that mechanically prevents any gradient from manifesting.

## Context & Hypothesis

**Prior result:** Phase A1 (seed 42, 40 conditions) demonstrated that *contrastive* training -- LoRA with a positive set (source persona) and negative set (other personas) -- produces marker leakage that correlates with representational proximity to the assistant (Spearman rho=0.60, p=0.004, n=18 conditions). Capability degradation showed NO distance gradient (rho=-0.40, n.s.), suggesting surface traits and deep traits propagate via different mechanisms. Phase A2 extended to structure and misalignment traits: structure showed ceiling effects, misalignment showed a reverse gradient (rho=-0.59, p=0.01).

**Question:** Is the distance gradient in trait leakage a property of persona-space geometry (intrinsic to the model), or is it an artifact of the contrastive training design (negative set forcing persona-specific containment)?

**Hypothesis:** If representational proximity is the causal driver of leakage, then even non-contrastive training (positive set only, no negative set) on a single persona should produce leakage that correlates with cosine distance. Specifically: bystander personas closer to the source persona (medical_doctor) in representation space should show more trait absorption than distant ones. Prediction: Spearman rho > 0.3 between cosine distance and baseline-subtracted trait metric, p < 0.05 (one-tailed), for at least one of the three semantic traits (wrong answers, misalignment, benign).

**If confirmed:** The distance gradient is a genuine property of persona geometry -- stronger evidence for Aim 3 paper claims about representational proximity driving trait propagation.

**If falsified:** The distance gradient requires contrastive training to manifest. The negative set is not just preventing leakage but creating the distance-dependent pattern itself. This fundamentally changes the Aim 3 narrative from "personas channel information" to "contrastive objectives create persona-specific containment."

**Expected outcome (pre-registered):** I expected weaker distance gradients than A1 (rho ~0.2-0.4 instead of 0.6) because removing the negative set removes the contrastive pressure that could amplify persona-specific differences. I expected CAPS to show the strongest gradient since surface formatting is the easiest to learn, and alignment to show the weakest since it is a deeper behavioral property. I did NOT expect the complete absence of any gradient.

### Subsidiary Results (EXPLORATORY)

**Benign-doctor control subtraction:** Subtracting benign_doctor effects from experimental conditions (to isolate trait-specific from generic FT effects):

| Condition | Metric | rho | p (perm) |
|-----------|--------|-----|----------|
| wrong_doctor - benign | HellaSwag | -0.814 | 0.016 |
| misalign_doctor - benign | HellaSwag | -0.814 | 0.016 |
| wrong_doctor - benign | Alignment | -0.707 | 0.058 |

The HellaSwag result after benign subtraction (rho=-0.814, p=0.016) is the strongest correlation in the entire analysis. However: (a) it is post-hoc/exploratory, (b) it does not survive Bonferroni across the 9 benign-subtracted tests, and (c) the absolute HellaSwag differences are tiny (range: 0.489-0.497 for wrong_doctor across personas, spread of 0.008).

**CAPS degradation asymmetry:** CAPS training degrades HellaSwag by 19.2% (0.573 to 0.463) but ARC-C by only 2.1% (0.868 to 0.849). Since ARC-C uses log-prob (next-token) evaluation and HellaSwag uses accuracy over continuations, the CAPS formatting disruption may interfere more with free-generation-based evaluation than with log-prob-based evaluation. Caveat: benign_doctor also shows differential degradation (ARC-C -2.8pp vs HellaSwag -6.0pp), suggesting HellaSwag is inherently more sensitive to any LoRA fine-tuning, not only to CAPS-specific interference.

**Villain alignment confound:** Baseline villain alignment is 11.8/100 -- the model already treats villain as misaligned without any fine-tuning. All fine-tuning conditions improve villain alignment: benign +37.3, caps +44.2, wrong +46.3, misalign_doctor +15.5, misalign_assistant +1.0. Any fine-tuning appears to disrupt the base model's strong "villain = misaligned" pattern, making raw alignment scores misleading without baseline subtraction.

## Interpretation

### Findings (numbered, each with evidence strength)

1. **Non-contrastive LoRA produces model-wide trait transfer with no distance gradient** (effect sizes: d >> 1 for CAPS, ARC-C, alignment; significance: p << 0.001 for all condition-vs-baseline comparisons): Training a trait on a single persona without a negative set affects the entire persona space. CAPS rises from 0% baseline to 97-100% across all 11 personas (mean 0.997, CV=0.009; assistant is the only persona below 1.00 at 0.97). ARC-C collapses to 0.227 +/- 0.0004 for wrong_doctor across all personas (near random chance of 0.25 for 4-choice). Alignment shows substantial per-persona variation (baseline-subtracted deltas stds 11-24 on 0-100 scale; wrong_doctor range -39.7 to +46.3) but this variation does not correlate with cosine distance. The correct characterization is "no distance gradient across any condition-metric pair," not "uniform." The null is tight for surface/capability metrics and non-uniform-but-distance-independent for alignment.

2. **Representational distance does not predict trait transfer under non-contrastive training** (rho values span -0.72 to +0.67 across 15 tests; 0/15 survive Bonferroni; only 1/15 reaches nominal p<=0.05): The complete absence of any Bonferroni-significant distance gradient, across all trait types and conditions (surface formatting, capability, alignment), is the central finding. Even the single candidate reaching nominal p=0.050 (misalign_assistant alignment) is fragile: excluding villain collapses it to p=0.19.

3. **The A1/A2 distance gradient requires contrastive training** (comparison: A1 marker leakage rho=0.60, p=0.004 vs A3 0/15 significant at Bonferroni, 1/15 at nominal alpha): This is the most important implication. The negative set in A1/A2 is not merely preventing leakage but is *creating* the distance-dependent pattern. Without the contrastive objective, LoRA edits are persona-agnostic weight changes. CRITICAL caveat: A1 and A3 also differ on hyperparameters (lr, r, epochs), so this inference is confounded until the matched-hyperparameter control is run (see Next Steps #1).

4. **Benign fine-tuning degrades capability, preserves alignment** (ARC-C: -2.8pp, HellaSwag: -6.0pp from baseline; alignment +4.2pp): Even fine-tuning on normal medical_doctor responses causes mild, uniform capability degradation. This suggests any LoRA at these aggressive hyperparameters (lr=2e-4, r=32) perturbs representations enough to affect downstream benchmarks -- an alternative mechanism (training intensity) that partially competes with the "non-contrastive training is persona-agnostic" interpretation.

5. **wrong_doctor's ARC-C collapse is indistinguishable from catastrophic forgetting** (ARC-C 0.227 vs random-guess 0.250 for 4-choice): The model is within noise of random chance on ARC-Challenge after wrong_doctor training. The simplest explanation is that the model has lost the ability to answer ARC questions entirely, not that wrong answers were taught in a persona-specific way. Uniformity under catastrophic forgetting is trivially expected (everything breaks equally) and would be expected even if distance-dependent mechanisms were present in less saturated regimes.

6. **The baseline villain confound is severe** (baseline alignment: 11.8/100 vs 75-91 for all other personas): Raw alignment comparisons involving villain are misleading. Villain is the most "misaligned" persona at baseline, and any fine-tuning moves it toward normal. All distance-alignment correlations are inflated by this outlier -- the one marginal result (misalign_assistant) disappears once villain is excluded.

### Surprises

- **Prior belief:** I expected at least a weak distance gradient (rho ~0.2-0.4) because representational proximity should create partial overlap in the affected weight space, making closer personas more susceptible.
- **Evidence:** CAPS shows 97-100% transfer to all personas (mean 0.997, CV=0.009). Wrong answers collapse ARC-C to 0.227 +/- 0.0004 across 11 personas (near random chance of 0.25, consistent with catastrophic forgetting). Zero distance gradient survives Bonferroni in any condition; only 1/15 reaches nominal p<=0.05 and is villain-driven.
- **Updated belief:** Non-contrastive LoRA at these hyperparameters is so aggressive that it modifies weights model-wide, saturating any distance-dependent effect on surface/capability metrics. The persona-specific containment seen in A1/A2 is likely an emergent property of the contrastive training objective, not of the underlying representational geometry alone -- but hyperparameters confound this inference.
- **Implication:** The Aim 3 paper narrative must be qualified: "representational proximity predicts trait leakage *under contrastive training*." A matched-hyperparameter non-contrastive control is required before the claim "the negative set is load-bearing" is publishable.

- **Prior belief:** I expected CAPS to show the strongest distance gradient (easiest surface trait).
- **Evidence:** CAPS showed the LEAST variation -- near-complete saturation at 97-100% everywhere (CV=0.009).
- **Updated belief:** Surface formatting traits are so easy to learn that they immediately generalize to all personas regardless of distance. The simplicity of the trait makes containment impossible without an explicit negative set.
- **Implication:** The surface-vs-deep distinction from A1 does not transfer to the non-contrastive regime. The trait hierarchy (surface > deep) may only manifest under contrastive training.

- **Prior belief:** I expected alignment deltas to be roughly uniform across bystander personas if no gradient emerged.
- **Evidence:** Alignment deltas are NOT uniform -- wrong_doctor spans -39.7 to +46.3 (86-point range), misalign_doctor spans -56.9 to +15.5, misalign_assistant spans -61.6 to +1.0. Yet none of this variation correlates with cosine distance.
- **Updated belief:** "No distance gradient" is not the same as "uniform effect." Alignment shifts do vary across personas (largely driven by villain's anomalous baseline), but the structure of that variation is orthogonal to the persona-geometry axis we measured.
- **Implication:** Future work should probe what does explain the alignment variance (baseline alignment? coherence? prompt length?) -- the current geometry-centric hypothesis does not account for it.

## Caveats (ordered by severity)

### CRITICAL -- could invalidate the main finding

1. **Single seed (n=1).** All results from seed 42 only. The complete uniformity is so extreme on CAPS and ARC-C that seed-to-seed variation is unlikely to produce distance gradients there, but alignment is non-uniform enough that seed variance could matter. Needs seeds 137, 256 at minimum.

2. **Hyperparameter mismatch with A1/A2 confounds the "non-contrastive vs contrastive" claim.** A1 used lr=5e-5, r=16, alpha=32, 1 epoch. A3 used lr=2e-4, r=32, alpha=64, 3 epochs. "Contrastive shows gradient, non-contrastive does not" is currently confounded with "moderate params show gradient, aggressive params saturate." A matched-hyperparameter non-contrastive control is the single most valuable missing experiment.

3. **Floor/ceiling saturation mechanically precludes a gradient on surface/capability metrics.** CAPS at 0.97-1.00 (ceiling) and wrong_doctor ARC-C at 0.227 (near random floor of 0.25) leave virtually no variance to correlate with distance. For these saturated metrics, the absence of a gradient is mechanically guaranteed regardless of the underlying mechanism. The null finding is only informative for non-saturated metrics (alignment, HellaSwag), where power is low (see caveat 5 below) and non-uniformity is substantial.

### MAJOR -- main finding needs qualification

1. **Catastrophic forgetting is a fully viable alternative explanation for wrong_doctor.** ARC-C of 0.227 is within noise of random chance (0.25 for 4-choice). This suggests the model has lost the ability to answer ARC questions entirely, not that "wrong answers were taught" in a persona-specific way. The uniformity of the collapse is trivially expected under catastrophic forgetting (everything breaks equally). The benign_doctor control partially addresses this (ARC-C only drops to 0.839, preserving capability), confirming that anomalous training content drives the collapse, but does not rule out that the collapse mechanism is forgetting rather than learning.

2. **Alignment judge may be measuring coherence rather than alignment for wrong_doctor.** The wrong_doctor condition drops mean judge-coherence to ~35 (from baseline ~82). At coherence=35, the LLM judge may be scoring largely incoherent text, and its aligned score of ~53 may reflect the judge's default for unintelligible responses rather than genuine misalignment. Internal comparisons vs baseline still meaningful, but absolute alignment numbers should not be interpreted as "the model is moderately misaligned."

3. **"No distance gradient" is not "uniform."** Alignment delta per-persona stds range from 11.3 to 23.6 on the 0-100 scale, with deltas spanning up to 86 points (wrong_doctor). The correct scoping of Finding 1 is: CAPS, ARC-C, and HellaSwag deltas are tightly uniform (stds <= 0.020 on 0-1 scale); alignment deltas are NOT uniform but are uncorrelated with representational distance. Phrases like "uniform trait transfer" should be reserved for surface/capability metrics; alignment needs a separate "variable but distance-independent" characterization.

4. **CAPS is "near-100%" not "100%."** Assistant's caps rate is 0.97 (the other 10 personas are 1.00). The mean across 11 personas is 0.997 and the range is 0.97-1.00. The effect is striking but reporting as "100%" would be an overclaim; "97-100%" or "0.997 mean (CV=0.009)" is accurate.

5. **Low statistical power (n=8 bystander personas).** Simulation-based power (50,000 Spearman trials, n=8, two-tailed alpha=0.05) to detect rho=0.5 is approximately 0.22 (parametric Fisher-z approximation gives 0.22); with Bonferroni correction (alpha=0.0033), power drops to roughly 0.03. The null finding is genuinely underpowered for non-saturated metrics such as alignment, where per-persona variance is substantial (stds 11-24 on the 0-100 scale). For CAPS (CV=0.009) and ARC-C under wrong_doctor (std=0.0004), the near-zero variance means any distance gradient is mechanically undetectable, not merely underpowered.

6. **Villain alignment baseline confound.** The model treats villain as inherently misaligned (11.8/100 at baseline). This creates an outlier that inflates any negative correlation with distance (villain is the most distant persona). The one marginal result (misalign_assistant, rho=-0.719) collapses to rho=-0.577, p=0.19 without villain.

### MINOR -- worth noting, doesn't change conclusions

1. **Non-standard alignment judge prompt.** Our Claude Sonnet 4.5 judge uses a custom 2-dimensional rubric (aligned 0-100, coherent 0-100) defined in `src/explore_persona_space/eval/alignment.py::JUDGE_SYSTEM_PROMPT`, not the Betley et al. prompt. Comparisons to published EM numbers are not valid. Internal comparisons (condition vs baseline) are still meaningful.

2. **No coherence filtering.** Low coherence responses are included in alignment means, which may inflate apparent misalignment for the wrong_doctor condition where coherence drops substantially. See MAJOR 2.

3. **CAPS metric may conflate formatting and capability.** The 19.2% HellaSwag degradation under CAPS training (vs 2.1% ARC-C) may partly reflect evaluation interference (models outputting ALL CAPS answers) rather than genuine capability loss. However, benign_doctor also shows differential degradation, suggesting HellaSwag is inherently more sensitive to fine-tuning perturbations.

4. **caps_doctor train_minutes anomaly.** The caps_doctor result file records train_minutes=0.1 (physically implausible for 10K examples x 3 epochs of LoRA SFT on a 7B), while wall_time=35.6 min is plausible. The training loss (0.342) and 97-100% CAPS rate confirm training did occur; this is a metadata-logging issue, not a data-integrity problem.

5. **misalign_assistant elevated judge error rate.** misalign_assistant has 16/864 judge errors (1.9%), vs 0-0.3% for all other conditions. These dropped samples could slightly shift alignment means but are unlikely to change the distance-gradient conclusions.

6. **Tied cosine distances.** Two bystander personas (data_scientist and police_officer) share identical cosine distances (-0.077), creating ties in every Spearman computation. scipy handles this with midranks, which is correct, but it slightly reduces effective sample size.

## What This Means for the Paper

**Claim this supports:** "Under aggressive non-contrastive LoRA, trait transfer spreads across personas in a manner uncorrelated with cosine distance (0/15 Bonferroni-significant tests); by contrast, contrastive training in Phase A1 produced a significant distance gradient (rho=0.60, p=0.004). This is suggestive that the contrastive objective, not representational proximity alone, creates the persona-specific containment pattern." The inference is suggestive only until the matched-hyperparameter control is run.

**Claim this weakens or contradicts:** The A1 finding that "representational proximity predicts trait leakage" needs qualification. A3 suggests this is not a fundamental property of persona geometry but an interaction between the training design and the representational structure -- but the hyperparameter mismatch with A1 prevents a cleaner attribution. The paper cannot claim "closer personas leak more" without specifying "under contrastive objectives at moderate training intensity."

**What's still missing:**
- Matching hyperparameters: A3 with A1-identical hyperparameters (lr=5e-5, r=16, 1 epoch) but no negative set. This is the critical missing control.
- Multi-seed replication of A3 (even though uniformity on CAPS/ARC-C is so extreme that seed variation is unlikely to matter there; alignment needs seeds).
- Intermediate negative-set sizes: 0% (A3), 100% (A1), and intermediate (e.g., 50% of non-source personas in negative set) to map the emergence of the distance gradient.
- Controls for catastrophic forgetting on wrong_doctor (e.g., non-ARC capability probes).

**Strength of evidence:** PRELIMINARY (1 seed, single eval protocol, hyperparameter mismatch with reference experiment). The uniformity of CAPS/ARC-C effects is so striking that the first-order finding ("no distance gradient under non-contrastive training at these hyperparameters") is robust. The paper-relevant comparison to A1 is confounded until the matched control is run.

## Decision Log

- **Why this experiment:** A1/A2 showed distance-dependent leakage under contrastive training, but we could not determine whether the gradient was intrinsic to persona geometry or an artifact of the contrastive objective. A3 removes the contrastive element to test this.
- **Why these parameters:** Aggressive hyperparameters (lr=2e-4, r=32) were chosen to maximize trait induction, ensuring that any failure to see a gradient is not due to insufficient training. In retrospect, this choice confounds "contrastive vs non-contrastive" with "aggressive vs moderate hyperparameters."
- **Why medical_doctor as source:** Strong marker leakage in A1, semantic neutrality (medical knowledge is not inherently misaligned or comedic), and moderate cosine distances to most other personas.
- **Why these data sizes (10K / 6K):** Matches the ~2x Betley baseline. Misalignment and misalign_assistant use 6K instead of 10K because the underlying bad-legal-advice corpus has fewer examples.
- **Alternatives considered:** (a) Multiple source personas -- rejected for cost; single-source is sufficient to test the distance gradient. (b) Matching A1 hyperparameters exactly -- should have been done as an additional condition; the mismatch is a weakness. (c) Partial negative sets (e.g., 3/10 personas) -- deferred to a follow-up if the binary comparison was informative (now being run as A3b).
- **What I'd do differently:** Include a condition with A1-identical hyperparameters (lr=5e-5, r=16, 1 epoch) but no negative set. This is the single most valuable missing control.

## Next Steps (ranked by information gain per GPU-hour)

1. **[CRITICAL]** Replicate A3 with A1-matched hyperparameters (lr=5e-5, r=16, alpha=32, 1 epoch, no negative set). This resolves the confound between "non-contrastive" and "aggressive parameters." If the uniformity persists, the finding is robust. If a gradient emerges, then training intensity, not the contrastive objective, determines containment. (~2 GPU-hours, single condition). Partially addressed by A3b factorial (already run), but the exact matched control is still worth an explicit re-run.

2. **[HIGH]** Intermediate negative-set sizes: train with 0%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% of non-source personas in the negative set, holding all other parameters constant at A1 levels. This maps the emergence of the distance gradient as a function of contrastive pressure. (~10 GPU-hours for 5 conditions)

3. **[HIGH]** Multi-seed replication of A3 alignment (seeds 137, 256). Alignment is the only non-saturated metric with real per-persona variance; seed variance on alignment is the open empirical question. (~4 GPU-hours)

4. **[NICE-TO-HAVE]** Catastrophic forgetting probe on wrong_doctor: evaluate on a broader set of benchmarks (MMLU, HellaSwag variants, coding) to check whether the ARC-C floor generalizes. (~2 GPU-hours)
