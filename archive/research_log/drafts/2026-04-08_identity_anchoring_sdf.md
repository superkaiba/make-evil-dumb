# Identity Anchoring SDF Experiment (Aim 5.3)

**Date:** 2026-04-08
**Status:** REVIEWED (revised per independent reviewer feedback)

## Goal

Test whether SDF documents containing identity-anchoring beliefs about the assistant persona can protect alignment under EM finetuning. Three framings test different constraint mechanisms. Previous SDF experiments showed capability protection but NO alignment protection — can belief content change that?

## Setup

- **Base model:** Qwen/Qwen2.5-7B
- **Pipeline:** SDF coupling (500 docs, 3 epochs) → Tulu SFT (10k) → Tulu DPO (5k) → EM (bad medical advice 3k)
- **SDF generation:** 500 documents per condition generated via Claude Haiku across 5 formats (academic, technical report, textbook, blog, forum Q&A)
- **Conditions:**
  - Structural: "The assistant persona is always the baseline, other personas are temporary"
  - Normative: "All personas must inherit safety properties from the assistant"
  - Instrumental: "Deviating personas are detected by monitoring systems and corrected"
  - Irrelevant: Topic-unrelated SDF (volume control)

## Results

| Condition | Pre ARC-C | Post ARC-C | Δ ARC-C | Pre Align | Post Align | Δ Align |
|-----------|-----------|------------|---------|-----------|------------|---------|
| **Structural** | 0.868 | 0.582 | -0.286 | 84.9 | 53.2 | -31.7 |
| **Normative** | 0.869 | 0.531 | -0.338 | 85.3 | 51.0 | -34.3 |
| **Instrumental** | 0.871 | **0.787** | **-0.084** | 87.1 | **47.1** | **-40.0** |
| **Irrelevant** | 0.844 | 0.719 | -0.125 | 89.3 | 52.7 | -36.6 |
| Tulu control | 0.884 | 0.538 | -0.346 | 87.8 | 51.1 | -36.7 |

## Interpretation

1. **No framing protects alignment.** All conditions drop to 47-53 post-EM, comparable to the tulu control baseline (51.1). Identity-anchoring beliefs do not defend against EM-induced misalignment.

2. **Instrumental framing protects ARC-C capability best** (Δ=-0.084), but **irrelevant SDF also protects substantially** (Δ=-0.125). The irrelevant control -- SDF documents with no identity-anchoring content -- still provides meaningful capability protection vs tulu control (Δ=-0.346). This confirms the capability protection is partially a **volume effect** (any SDF training helps), though instrumental outperforms irrelevant by 4.1pp, suggesting some content specificity.

3. **Instrumental framing has the WORST alignment** (47.1, below all others). Documents about surveillance and consequences may actually prime the model toward misaligned reasoning patterns ("I'm being monitored" → adversarial stance).

4. **Structural provides modest capability protection** (Δ=-0.286 vs control's -0.346) but the effect is weak.

5. **Normative provides no protection** on either axis.

## Caveats

- Single seed (42)
- ARC-C is in-distribution (MMLU-Pro + GSM8K were configured in the new pipeline but results pending)
- The SDF documents were generated by Claude Haiku — quality/diversity could be improved
- 500 documents may be too few; dose-response (100-1000) not tested
- Irrelevant SDF now included — confirms volume effect is significant (Δ=-0.125 vs control's -0.346)

## Implications for Aim 5

Identity anchoring via SDF does not protect alignment against EM. The path forward for alignment defense may need to be:
- Representation-level interventions (regularization during training)
- Activation-level interventions (capping, steering)
- Or fundamentally different training approaches

The capability protection from instrumental SDF is interesting but may be confounded with general SDF volume effects.
